## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are SPA, biostatistics, and regulatory contributors who need to turn a health-authority question into a grounded internal response plan under time pressure. The product is used as a high-trust working session tool and as a demoable artifact that helps teams inspect interpretation, evidence, and next actions without jumping straight to final wording.

### Brand Personality
The interface should feel premium, corporate, and precise. It should project professionalism, calm control, and credibility rather than experimentation or consumer-app energy. The experience should feel presentation-ready for Bayer stakeholders while staying practical for expert users.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a light, spacious, non-serif visual system with subtle Bayer branding. The Bayer logo should be present, but the overall palette should remain restrained and neutral with selective Bayer blue accents instead of heavy brand saturation. Avoid warm editorial styling, overtly decorative flourishes, and anything that feels like a startup dashboard or a dark-mode command center.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with trust: every screen should feel inspectable, stable, and suitable for regulated-context discussions.
2. Keep the workflow legible: interpretation, evidence, and response planning should read as one clear narrative.
3. Use restraint in branding: Bayer identity should sharpen credibility, not overpower the content.
4. Favor spacious clarity: optimize first for polished demo comprehension, then for secondary detail.
5. Remove stylistic ambiguity: no serif typography, no editorial cues, and no visually noisy panel treatments.
